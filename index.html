What I learned about IL taxes from Will Guzzardi.

Will Guzzardi is a State Representative for Illinois's 39th District.
We had a good discussion about taxes on Twitter.
Here's what I learned from him, with supporting notes and illustrations.
Will:   “Our income tax is *very* low.”
Notes:
This is the Illinois income tax rate.
3.75%
Source: IL Department of Revenue
Will:   “If we taxed at Wisconsin rates we'd have $10 BILLION more, 2x the current deficit.”
Notes:
These are Wisconsin's income tax rates.
4.00%
5.84%
6.27%
7.65%
Source: Wisconsin Department of Revenue
Will:   “It's bad in Chicago, @alexsoble, but way worse in poor communities with little property value. (see: Harvey, East St Louis, et al) ”
Notes:
Will:   “But if we want to save money, @alexsoble, let's invest in diversion, mental health, community care to keep ppl out of jail/ER/nursing homes”
Notes:
Keeping people in jail is expensive. Take the case of Lamont Cathey, a Cook County jail inmate who has cost the county more than $1 million in health care costs alone. Lamont was arrested for the theft of money from a pizzeria safe, jailed for not being able to post a $5,000 bond, and has been hospitalized two dozen times since his arrest.
